# Privacy Policy for ScreenSort

**Last Updated: April 16, 2026**

This Privacy Policy describes how ScreenSort ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and shares your personal information when you use our desktop application and services (the "Service").

## 1. Information We Collect

### 1.1 Information from Google OAuth
When you sign in to ScreenSort using your Google Account, we receive certain information from Google, including:
*   **Email Address**: Used to identify your account and provide support.
*   **Full Name**: Used to personalize your experience within the application.
*   **Profile Picture**: Used to display your avatar in the application's profile section.

### 1.2 Local Data (Metadata)
ScreenSort is primarily an offline-first application. We collect metadata, including:
*   **HotKey Mappings & Settings**: To develop ergonomic features of the application.
*   **Usage Statistics**: Such as number of screenshots taken, to provide you with insights.

**IMPORANT**: ScreenSort **does not** upload, store, or have access to your actual screenshot image files. All images are stored locally on your device in the directories you specify.

## 2. How We Use Your Information
We use the information we collect to:
*   Authenticate your identity and manage your account.
*   Personalize your user profile within the app.
*   Synchronize your preferences and organization metadata across devices.
*   Provide customer support and respond to your inquiries.

## 3. Data Storage and Security
*   **Authentication**: Managed securely by AWS Services.
*   **Local Files**: Your screenshots remain on your local hard drive and are never transmitted to our servers.

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your metadata and authentication tokens.

## 4. Sharing Your Information
We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personal information to third parties. We only share information with service providers (like AWS and Google) as necessary to provide the Service.

## 5. Your Rights
You have the right to:
*   Access the personal information we hold about you.
*   Request the deletion of your account and associated metadata.
*   Withdraw your consent for Google OAuth at any time via your Google Account settings.
